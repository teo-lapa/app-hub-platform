## Introduction
Seasonal menus leverage products at peak freshness and economy. Restaurants with seasonal menus see food cost -15-25%, customer satisfaction +20-30%.

## Advantages
**Economic**: Costs -20-40%, Tomatoes summer 6 CHF/kg vs winter 14 CHF/kg
**Quality**: Superior flavor, maximum freshness
**Marketing**: Perceived novelty, urgency, storytelling
**Sustainability**: Reduced environmental impact, local support

## Swiss Seasonal Ingredients
**Spring**: Asparagus, peas, wild garlic, spinach
**Summer**: Tomatoes, zucchini, eggplant, berries
**Autumn**: Pumpkin, porcini, chestnuts, grapes
**Winter**: Cabbage, celeriac, Jerusalem artichoke, radicchio

## Implementation Strategy
**Menu Structure**: Core 60-70% (always available) + Seasonal 30-40% (3-4 month rotation)
**Timeline**: 10 weeks before planning, 6 weeks photos/design, 2 weeks training
**Pricing**: Value-based, seasonal ingredients justify equal/higher prices
**Marketing**: Email, social media, in-loco communication

## Operational Management
**Inventory**: Small quantities frequently (2-3x/week), strict FIFO
**Staff Training**: 2-3 hours complete team training before launch
**KPIs**: Sales per seasonal dish, Food cost %, Customer feedback
